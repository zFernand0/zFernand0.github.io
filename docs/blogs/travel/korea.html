🇰🇷 South Korea Adventure

September 5th - 8th, 2025

Trip Overview

This high-intensity itinerary is designed for travelers eager to experience Seoul and a taste of Busan in just four days. The chart below provides a quick visual summary of your activity allocation, showcasing a trip packed with cultural sights, culinary delights, and significant travel.

T-Money Card: Purchase upon arrival for seamless public transport (subway, bus).

Navigation Apps: Use Naver Map or KakaoMap as Google Maps functionality is limited in Korea.

Currency: Korean Won (KRW). Credit cards are widely accepted, but cash is useful for small vendors.

Pre-Bookings: Secure your Nanta performance and DMZ tour tickets well in advance. KTX tickets are also recommended for pre-booking.

Hanbok Rental: Wearing a traditional hanbok can grant you free entry to palaces like Gyeongbokgung.

Day 1: Friday, September 5 – Arrival in Seoul & Early Night

📍 Seoul Hotel (Jung-gu or Yongsan-gu)

🛬

2:30 PM (KST): Land at Incheon International Airport (ICN)

Clear customs and pick up your pre-booked SIM card or pocket WiFi.

🚆

2:30 PM – 4:00 PM: Travel to hotel near Seoul Station

Take the AREX train or bus to your hotel in the Jung-gu or Yongsan-gu area.

🏨

4:00 PM – 5:00 PM: Hotel check-in

Check in, drop off your luggage, and take a moment to freshen up.

🛍️

5:00 PM – 7:00 PM: Myeongdong Street Food Tour

Immerse yourself in the bustling Myeongdong district, trying various Korean street foods and exploring the cosmetic shops.

🎭

7:00 PM – 9:00 PM: Nanta Performance (8:00 PM show)

Head to the Nanta Theatre in Myeongdong for an energetic, non-verbal show. Remember to book your tickets in advance!

🛌

9:00 PM: Return to hotel and rest

Head back to the hotel for a good night's sleep to prepare for the early start tomorrow.

Day 2: Saturday, September 6 – Busan Whirlwind Day Trip

📍 Seoul to Busan and back

🚄

6:00 AM – 9:30 AM: KTX to Busan

Travel to Seoul Station and board the high-speed KTX train. The ride takes about 2.5 hours. Consider booking a First Class ticket for more comfort.

🎨

10:30 AM – 1:30 PM: Gamcheon Culture Village

After arriving at Busan Station, store your luggage in a locker. Take public transport to the village and explore its vibrant, cascading houses and murals.

🐟

1:30 PM – 4:30 PM: Jagalchi Fish Market & Lunch

Head to Jagalchi Market for a fresh seafood lunch and a walk through the lively stalls. If you have time, you can also explore nearby Gukje Market.

🌆

4:30 PM – 9:30 PM: Return to Seoul

Travel back to Busan Station, grab your luggage, and take the KTX back to Seoul. Enjoy dinner at Seoul Station or have a light meal on the train.

Day 3: Sunday, September 7 – DMZ Full Day Tour

📍 Seoul (DMZ Tour)

🛡️

7:00 AM – 5:00 PM: Full-Day DMZ Tour

Join a pre-booked guided tour to the Demilitarized Zone, which includes sites like Imjingak Park, the Third Infiltration Tunnel, and the Dorasan Observatory. Lunch is typically included or available as an option.

🌙

5:00 PM onwards: Return to Seoul & Dinner

After the tour, you'll be dropped off back in Seoul. Enjoy a nice dinner and get an early night's rest.

Day 4: Monday, September 8 – Palaces & Departure

📍 Seoul (Final sights & travel)

🏛️

8:30 AM – 12:30 PM: Royal Palaces Tour

Check out of your hotel and store your luggage. Head to Gyeongbokgung Palace and aim to see the 10:00 AM Royal Guard-Changing Ceremony. Spend time exploring the palace grounds.

🍜

12:30 PM – 1:30 PM: Lunch near Gyeongbokgung or in Insadong

Enjoy one last delicious Korean meal.

🏘️

1:30 PM – 4:00 PM: Traditional Seoul

Take a final stroll through the charming streets of Insadong and visit the serene Jogyesa Buddhist Temple.

✈️

4:00 PM – 6:30 PM: Travel to ICN Airport

Head back to your hotel to retrieve your luggage and then take the AREX to Incheon International Airport (ICN).

🛫

6:30 PM onwards: Check-in & Departure

Go through airport procedures, enjoy a final dinner, and do some last-minute duty-free shopping before your flight departs at 10:00 PM.
